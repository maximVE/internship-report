openthebox operates in the B2B SaaS sector, providing an AI-powered business intelligence platform aggregating data on over 3 million Belgian companies. Founded in 2017 by Niek Bartholomeus and later acquired by media group Mediafin, the platform centralises public financial data — from the NBB, KBO, and De Tijd — into one fast, visual dashboard enriched with AI insights.

Their audience is financial professionals: investment analysts, private equity firms, M&A teams, and banks who need fast, reliable company intelligence for high-stakes decisions. Communicating to this audience means designing for trust, clarity, and confidence — every visual choice must serve the data, not distract from it.

Through three months here I learned what it means to build a visual language for a data product — translating complex financial intelligence into motion and video content that feels intuitive, premium, and distinctly Belgian-tech.

05 / Critical Reflection

internship & programme evaluation

Three Positives

Creative freedom. openthebox trusted me with genuine ownership over the visual direction of their content. Rare for an intern — it pushed my skills further than a constrained role ever could.

Exceptional team culture. The people made it a genuinely enjoyable, stimulating environment. A positive team directly affects creative output — I felt supported, not just managed.

Entry into dynamic design. First real professional exposure to motion design as a discipline. Exploring something genuinely unknown is something I'll carry into my career.

Three Improvements

Limited learning curve. As the primary designer on many projects, there weren't more experienced motion designers to learn from directly. Self-teaching is valuable, but mentorship accelerates growth in ways solo work cannot.

Inconsistent feedback on design. Feedback cycles were sometimes sparse or delayed. Without regular direction it was harder to calibrate early — especially in the first weeks.

Small design team. Limited exposure to collaborative design processes — peer iteration, design reviews, cross-functional sessions. These are experiences to seek out in future roles.

IGM Programme ↔ Professional Practice

The IGM programme gave me a strong grounding in visual principles, layout, typography, and design thinking — all directly applicable from day one. The gap was most visible in motion design and video production: the programme covers these lightly, but professional standard demands deep technical mastery of After Effects. The biggest added value of this internship was precisely this — three months of applied, high-stakes practice in a specialism no classroom alone could have given me.

06 / Final Summary

closing reflection

This internship at openthebox (IGM-3) was a three-month deep dive into motion design within a B2B SaaS environment, where I worked on video content, UI animation, and reusable visual systems.

The most important shift in my thinking was moving from designing isolated visuals to designing systems that scale across formats. Every project required consistency, clarity, and trust-driven communication.

On a personal level, I learned how to work through iteration-heavy creative processes, handle feedback professionally, and make design decisions with confidence and reasoning instead of hesitation.

This experience confirmed my interest in motion design and strengthened my ability to operate in real production environments where speed, clarity, and precision matter.
